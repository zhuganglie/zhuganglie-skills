# Subject Scoping and Periodization

Use this reference when the subject is broad, ambiguous, or difficult to split into phases.

## 1. Choose the Right Unit of Analysis

Many user prompts hide multiple possible subjects. Pick the one that best matches the question.

- "AI history" can mean research field, commercial industry, infrastructure stack, or public discourse.
- "Streaming history" can mean media distribution model, consumer habit, or platform industry.
- "Coffee history" can mean commodity chain, cafe culture, consumer product, or colonial trade system.

When multiple units are plausible, select one and state it explicitly.

## 2. What Justifies a New Phase

A phase break is warranted when the dominant logic changes. Common triggers:

- New enabling technology or production method
- New cost structure or infrastructure
- New regulatory classification or standard
- New mass audience, user segment, or adoption channel
- New dominant business model
- New cultural meaning or prestige position

Do not split phases just because the calendar changed.

## 3. Subject-Type Heuristics

- Technology: phase around capability jumps, standards, and commercialization.
- Industry: phase around production, distribution, market concentration, and revenue model.
- Institution: phase around authority, jurisdiction, and enforcement capacity.
- Cultural form: phase around audience, status, medium, and meaning.
- Product category: phase around manufacturing, pricing, retail channel, and symbolic identity.

## 4. Inflection Point Test

An event is a real turning point only if it altered at least one of these:

- What actors could do
- What actors were rewarded for doing
- Who could participate
- How the subject was defined or governed

If it merely accelerated an existing direction without changing the rules, treat it as supporting context, not a headline turning point.

## 5. Architect Selection Test

Avoid lists made entirely of famous founders. Try to cover different mechanisms:

- Invention or discovery
- Standard setting or regulation
- Organizational scaling
- Distribution or infrastructure control
- Narrative, branding, or cultural legitimation

If a committee, agency, exchange, lab, or company changed the field more than any one person, name the organization instead of forcing a person.
